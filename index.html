Anna Pearl Wright

Operations Leader

I've led through crises that didn't have a playbook — not just solving for the moment, but building for what came after it.

Over seven years at SteadyMD, I grew from a twenty-one-year-old generalist into SVP of Operations — and was part of navigating every major pivot the company went through: hyper-growth, fundraising, acquisition, integration. Across every role the through-line has been the same: come in, find the levers, build something that holds.

I've done this in genuine crises and in slow burns. With formal authority and without it. In brand new problems with no framework and in broken systems with too much history. And what I've learned is that the technical fix is rarely the whole problem — the burned-out team that became the strongest in the company; the executive team that started functioning as one; the clients we almost lost who are now stable — none of that happens from systems alone.

I work at the intersection of systems thinking and relational intelligence. I know how to find the lever that moves the system. I also know that people have to be brought along for it to stay moved.
